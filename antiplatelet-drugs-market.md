<p><p>The antiplatelet drugs market encompasses various medication types used to prevent blood clots in conditions like cardiovascular diseases. Aspirin, a widely used first-line therapy, inhibits platelet aggregation. Clopidogrel and Ticagrelor are advanced options providing stronger effects, often for patients with acute coronary syndromes. Prasugrel offers rapid platelet inhibition, while Dipyridamole has dual action as an antiplatelet and vasodilator. Ticlopidine is an older agent with similar effects. Additionally, Abciximab and Tirofiban are glycoprotein IIb/IIIa inhibitors used during procedures to enhance antiplatelet effects.</p></p>
