<p><p>The Aerospace Auxiliary Power Unit (APU) is a crucial component that provides energy for various functions in aircraft when the main engines are not operating. It typically falls into two market types: Shaft Power Output Type, which uses mechanical energy to drive systems like hydraulic pumps or generators; and Compressed Air Output Type, which supplies high-pressure air for engine start and environmental control. Both types enhance operational efficiency, reduce reliance on external ground power, and ensure aircraft systems remain functional.</p></p>
